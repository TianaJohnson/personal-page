Cantina Groove


The Problem: I worked with a local bicycle frame builder to solve his organization and communication problem. He Is working with a local bike shop that provides professional bicycle fits for customers. This bike shop would be acting as a front end business to the bicycle builder. The bike shop would meet with customers, get detailed project information and size a customer on a “fit” bike to get proper measurements and then pass that information to the bike builder. The problem was that there was no concise or organized form of communication.

The Solution: I created a secure platform for a user to login, input customer contact information along with that customers project information. A user can add, edit, update and archive a customer from the dashboard. There is even a page for the users to communicate about status updates. This should help the client stay organized and on top of his work load.

Gaard One

Customer Platform


Gaard One is a unique company that sells modular outdoor equipment, giving all the profits to whichever concervincy they are currently working with. This helps the concervincies to grow. For every dollar a customer spends on a product, that dollar buys one foot of land thus reflecting the 1 to 1 moto of Gaard One.

Check them out here!

The Problem: The company wanted to create a way to interact further with there consumers outside of the purchasing of products, but they didn’t have away to do that.

The Solution: A team of 5 developers created an app using QR codes to increase customer engagement. When purchusing a product from Gaard One, A consumer will also recieve a QR code. Upon scanning, the QR code will take the customer to a user platform that shows the amount of land they helped save.
